### Deviations accepted (lead review)
1. **terrain `BALLAST_DROP = −0.4`** (signed). O8 (`terrainHeight==formation+BALLAST_DROP`)
   + O8b (`formation ≥ terrainHeight`) are jointly satisfiable only if ≤0. Ground/ballast
   shoulder sits 0.4 m below rail top (rail proud of ballast). *Instructed at kickoff.*
2. **terrain tunnel BORE-CARVE (significant, agent-originated, ACCEPTED).** The literal D27
   formula `terrainHeight=lerp(normGround,natural,m(s))` is d-independent, so inside a
   tunnel band (natural = hilltop, ABOVE formation) it puts the hilltop at `d=0` near the
   track — which (a) violates O8b ("track never below ground"), and (b) would make
   `clampEye` teleport the eye ~60 m up onto the hilltop inside the tunnel. This is a **real
   internal inconsistency in the HLD** between the D27 formula and O8b/clampEye for tunnel
   bands. The implementer resolved it correctly: a smooth, macro-only carve pulls the
   near-track corridor (|d|≤SHOULDER=4, entirely within the render-omitted |d|<BORE_HALF=6
   corridor) back to the bed = the bore floor — the physical "the line BORES the hill"
   model. Predicates still read the UN-carved hill (so `tunnelBoreAt` fires); no cliff
   (dense Δs=1 O8-edge sweep across both bands passes). **Lead verdict: correct and
   necessary** — to be adversarially re-verified in REVIEW (smoothness, predicate-firing,
   clampEye sanity).
3. **terrain O-DOMAIN bound** loosened from `<1` to `<2·eps+0.1` for `planarPoseAt.x/z` at
   the ends — the spine genuinely advances ~1 m per 1 m Δs (continuous motion, not a jump).
   The `0.1` constant looks loose; **flagged for REVIEW** to confirm it's not masking a real
   discontinuity (recommend tightening to a jump-style bound).
4. centerline O3 C0 test `eps=1e-8` (slope contribution ≈1.2e-10 < 1e-9; measures the
   discontinuity, not legitimate slope) — sound. camera scale=0 `=== 0` for −0 (Object.is
   artifact; value still exact zero) — sound. P5 bloomStrength peaks at *rain* not storm so
   night×rain is the unique global max per HLD §2.3 — sound.

### DEFECT FOUND & FIXED (lead) — far-horizon band occluded the viaduct valley
First viaduct screenshots showed **flat green at deck level — no valley** (while the tunnel
hill rendered fine). A throwaway probe confirmed the PURE model is correct
(`terrainHeight(8050,d)≈−44…−48`, 45 m below the formation; tunnel rises to +33), so the bug
was render-side. Root cause: `terrain-mesh.ts buildFarHorizon` built a **flat `RingGeometry`
at ~deck level (`y≈−1.6`) centred on the route midpoint, radius 490–1400 m** — the camera
physically stands on that annulus near mid-route, so it **occludes any terrain below deck
level** (the −44 m valley) while the +33 m tunnel hill pokes above it. A single flat band
cannot sit correctly for terrain ranging +33 to −48 m. *(First AB test was inconclusive —
Vite served a stale module; a clean dev-server restart confirmed: with the band gone, the
deck-over-valley appears.)* **Fix:** removed `buildFarHorizon` (call + function + the
`HORIZON_REACH` const). Fog already fades the ribbon edge into haze on every preset
(re-checked cutting/embankment/coast in day/clear — the edge reads fine). tsc clean, 242
green, build green after the fix.

### Confirmed findings — ALL FIXED (lead, surgical)
1. **MAJOR — O8c was a tautology** (`terrainHeight()` compared to a re-call of itself;
   also sampled `d∈[-3,0,3]` while piers sit at `±2.2`). Rewrote it to a real,
   render-independent oracle: at each pier `(s, ±PIER_OFFSET=2.2)` deep in the Wyre band,
   `terrainHeight ≈ naturalGround` (no carve on a viaduct) AND `< formationHeight −
   VIADUCT_THRESH` (a genuine drop, not the bed) + non-vacuity. (findings 1 & 3)
2. **O8 exact-float fragility** through `lerp(_,_,1)`. Fixed at the SOURCE: `lerp` now
   short-circuits endpoints (`t=0⇒p`, `t=1⇒q`) so the m=1/w=1 ground equality is
   bit-exact everywhere (hardens production, not just the test).
3. **O-DOMAIN displacement→jump bound.** Now subtracts the expected `2·eps·tangent`
   advance and requires residual `<1e-3` — detects a real discontinuity regardless of
   endpoint curvature (the old `<2eps+0.1` bound was sound only because both ends are κ=0).
4. **Bloom per-frame retry storm.** Added a permanent `bloomFailed` latch so a persistent
   lazy-import failure can't re-issue 4 `import()`s + a `Promise.all` every frame.
5. **Dead `void macroReliefAt`** with a comment claiming a guard that didn't exist (and a
   real guard there would be wrong — `sMid` falls in the tunnel band but the sea is offset).
   Dropped it + the now-unused import.
6. **`disposeTree` leaked materials** — now disposes materials (and arrays), not just
   geometries, so the teardown path is correct if ever wired.
