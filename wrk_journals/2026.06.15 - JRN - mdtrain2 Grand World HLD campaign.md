## Kickoff — scope (Arthur, via AskUserQuestion)
- **Centerpiece:** a real **living landscape** — replace the flat ground plane with
  a terrain **heightfield** the line genuinely threads through (cuttings that cut,
  embankments that carry the line above the fields, a river valley on a **viaduct**).
- **Look:** **push toward realism** — PBR materials/textures, richer meshes,
  post-processing (tone-mapping / bloom / shadows); still a **static-hostable**
  in-browser build (Pages); mobile best-effort, must not break.
- **Route:** **design a new grand route** — a longer, multi-chapter signature line
  (city terminus → suburb → countryside → viaduct → coast → tunnel → terminus), each
  chapter with a set-piece.

**Binding tension to resolve in the design:** "realism + grand route + heightfield"
vs the QUALITY BRIEF's "do not overengineer; hold it in your head; static-hostable;
mobile must not break; minimize env vars / no feature flags." The drafts + judges +
devil's advocate must keep this honest.

**Prime architectural smell (lead, pre-flagged):** today the camera and ALL track/
lineside geometry treat the line as a straight **+Z axis** (`z = chainage`, camera at
`(EYE_X,1.9,chainage-0.6)`). A curved, graded route through terrain needs a pure
**chainage → world {position, tangent, up}** centreline that BOTH sim (gradient) and
render (camera/track/terrain) derive from. That mapping is the likely prime testable
seam.
