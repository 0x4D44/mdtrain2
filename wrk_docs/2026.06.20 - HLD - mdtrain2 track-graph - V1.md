# HLD — Track Graph & Reactive AI (V1)

Status: DRAFT (V1, panel synthesis) · 2026.06.20 · additive over the Phase-1/2 core
Repo: `mdtrain2` ("The Night Cab") · Trunk: `origin/master` · Version on implement: **0.12.0 → 0.13.0** (minor; new pure capability)

> **Thesis.** A `TrackGraph` is the existing single-route geometry with the
> integration origin un-hardwired: one new field `EdgeFrame{psi0,x0,z0,h0}` turns
> today's `route` into an `Edge`, and the *already-tested* arc-compounding "the next
> curvature segment starts from the running ψ₀ and accumulated (x,z)" (oracle **O5b**)
> is lifted one level to "the next **edge** starts from its parent's exit frame".
> Trains carry `{edgeId,s,d}` instead of a scalar chainage; a thin pure `stepOnGraph`
> wraps the **unchanged** `step()` to carry the residual across an edge join.
> Occupancy is a *derived set* fed into the *existing* `aspectAt` cascade — a richer
> source for the same R→Y→YY→G machine, no new signal primitive. AI is a pure
> `aiInputs(...)` producing the same `SimInputs` the player's `resolveInputs` produces.
> Seven new pure concepts; no interlocking UI, no pathfinding, no rewrite of the asset.

---

## 0. Synthesis provenance (how V1 was built)

V1 takes the **simplest-viable** draft as its base (unanimous panel winner, 8.5/9/9:
the only draft that touches **zero tested lines** of `centerline.ts` and designs the
junction sign-reconciliation hazard out of existence by all-forward authoring), and
grafts the panel's strongest ideas from the runners-up:

| Graft | From | What it buys |
|---|---|---|
| **Differential join oracle (G-DIFF)** | testability-first | Pins `stepOnGraph`'s residual arithmetic against an *independent* construction (a concatenated single `Route`), not self-consistency — the single best oracle in the panel. Headline join test. |
| **Honest O17 fence** | testability-first | Split centerline-importing geometry (`graph-geom.ts`, the sanctioned spatial consumer) from the centerline-free 1-D logic (`graph.ts` types, `graph-step.ts`, `ai.ts`, `multitrain.ts`) so the physics-path import fence is a *true* statement, then add the 1-D modules to it. |
| **Clear-block token occupancy** | risk-first / testability-first | `aspectAt` source = served-stations ∪ clear-block-tokens (positive membership), dropping simplest-viable's `guardedStationOfEdge`-deletes-a-station indirection. |
| **O-STEP-PARITY** | risk-first | `stepOnGraph` bit-identical to a direct `step()` on a single-edge identity graph — proves the wrapper is a literal no-op on the legacy case. |
| **Risk-register spine** | risk-first | Every risky seam owns a named build-failing oracle, auditable row-by-row (§2). |
| **Deterministic lower-trainId tie-break** | risk-first | Closes the mutual-yield *wedge* the other two judges flagged, while staying order-independent. |
| **Truer pass-in-the-loop scenario + branch AI** | testability-first | AI held *in the loop* while the player on the main holds the shared onward block (genuine overtake), + a second AI on the branch for divergence — a real "looped to be passed", not a same-Path follower-hold. |
| **`validateGraph` derive + positive-control** | testability-first / risk-first | Continuity true *by construction* (child frame derived from `exitFrame(parent)`), with a perturb-and-reject test proving the check has teeth. |

**Rejected by the whole panel (the "M8 on-ramp to nowhere"):** testability-first's
`reversed[]` reverse-through-a-join machinery (per-Path reversed boolean, s′=length−residual,
speed/dir sign flip, mirror-route oracle). The increment never needs reverse running across a
junction; it is a deferred non-goal (§1.2) precisely so the sign-reconciliation surface stays
collapsed to one line.

## 1. Scope & non-goals

### 1.1 In scope (the first acceptance-tested increment)

A **testbed graph** with a through main, a **passing loop**, and a **junction to a
branch**, carrying **deterministic trains**:

- the **PLAYER**, driving a booked `Path` (an ordered list of edge ids) the scenario
  fixes — the player runs the through main and **overtakes** an AI that is **looped**;
  there is **no manual point-setting and no interlocking UI**;
- one **AI service** booked via the **passing loop**, driven by a pure target-speed
  tracker, that the player **delays by occupying the shared onward block** (the player
  on the main holds the block the looped AI must exit into → the AI's loop-exit signal
  goes RED → it brakes to a stand and waits *in the loop* until the player passes);
- a **second AI service** booked onto the **branch**, demonstrating divergence and the
  deterministic contention tie-break.

The increment delivers: the new pure types (`Edge`/`EdgeFrame`, `TrackGraph`,
`TrainPosition`, `Path`), the frame-aware geometry wrappers, the derived occupancy
folded into `aspectAt` as clear-block tokens, the pure `aiInputs(...)`, the pure
`stepOnGraph(...)`, an order-independent multi-train tick, `validateGraph`, and AI
trains rendered as world meshes via the existing `placeOnCentreline`/`placeOnEdge`
primitive.

### 1.2 Non-goals (deferred — explicitly NOT in this increment)

- **No interlocking UI, no signaller, no runtime pathfinding.** Routes are DATA; points
  are baked into the scenario `Path`s.
- **No reverse running across a junction.** A reversing player runs `s` back down the
  *current* edge and clamps at `s=0` (exactly as today); `dir = −1` never crosses an edge
  join. (This is the rejected `reversed[]` surface — designed out, not built.)
- **No timetable / platform dwell / station-stop logic for AI.** The AI tracks a speed
  target and obeys signals; it does not stop to *serve* platforms. A looped AI just holds
  at its loop-exit red until the onward block clears (D-DEC-4).
- **No `d` as a routing degree of freedom.** `TrainPosition.d` exists (the
  `placeOnCentreline` third argument) but is a **constant render offset only**. Parallel
  running is two distinct edges, never two `d` lanes on one edge (D-DEC-8).
- **No turnout geometry, no moving-blade meshes, no flange/check rails.** A node is a pure
  frame hand-off; divergence is baked into the *branch child edge's curvature* (D-DEC-6).
- **No new safety primitive for AI.** AI trains read the aspect and obey a braking curve;
  they do not run the AWS/TPWS/DSD machine (D-DEC-7). The player keeps the full Phase-2
  machine unchanged.
- **No multi-edge occupancy footprint, no train-length sweep across joins.** Occupancy
  granularity is **whole-edge**, keyed off a train's *current* `edgeId` plus a one-edge
  look-ahead reservation (D-DEC-3); a train's body is a point at `s` for occupancy.
- **No change to physics `step()`, `acceleration`, the AWS/TPWS/DSD machine, the
  latch-release contract, or any existing oracle.** Those are the asset; reused verbatim.

## 2. Risk register — the verification spine (every seam owns a build-failing oracle)

| # | Risk (failure mode) | Mitigation (design) | Named oracle (fails the build) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R1** | EdgeFrame lift regresses the geometry kernel | Geometry is a *rigid post-transform wrapper*; `centerline.ts` byte-untouched | **P0** (identity ≈ legacy, 12 dp), **P1** (composition = O5b lifted, 6 dp) |
| **R2** | Junction join carries the residual / world pose wrong | All-forward authoring; residual→positive `s`, `dir=+1`; no sign flip | **G-DIFF** (differential vs concatenated route, 1e-9), **J1–J3** |
| **R3** | `stepOnGraph` wrapper changes legacy single-edge behaviour | Wrapper is a no-op when no successor / identity frame | **O-STEP-PARITY** (bit-identical to `step()`), **P0** |
| **R4** | Edge frames drift apart at a join (discontinuity) | `validateGraph` derives child frame from `exitFrame(parent)` | **P2** (continuity ≤1e-6), **P2-NEG** (perturb 1e-3 ⇒ reject) |
| **R5** | Occupancy hold doesn't make the protecting signal RED | Clear-block token removed from `aspectAt` source set ⇒ RED via unchanged cascade | **OCC2** (held RED), **OCC1** (token set correct) |
| **R6** | Occupancy hold never releases (stale state / latch bug) | Occupancy is *re-derived every tick*, never stored; no latch touched | **OCC3** (release on clear), **latch contract untouched** |
| **R7** | Multi-train tick is order-dependent (non-deterministic) | Occupancy sampled once from frame-N positions; tie-break on stable `trainId` | **MT1** (permutation-invariant), **MT2** (deterministic contention) |
| **R8** | AI doesn't stop at a red (overruns the signal) | Braking-curve ceiling `v=√(2·a·d)` drives target→0 at the post | **AI2** (stand at/before post), **AI3** (proceeds on clear) |
| **R9** | A new sim module imports the spatial core / a clock | G3 grep globs `src/sim/*`; O17 fence extended to the 1-D graph modules | **G3** (existing grep), **O17** (extended import fence) |
| **R10** | Per-edge route invariants silently violated on a new edge | Per-route invariants run **per-edge** across `graph.edges` | **P3** (radius≥250 / ribbon / κ=0 bands / cant-off-ceiling per edge) |

and **S2** (branch divergence), §5.

## 3. The design

### 3.0 Why this is additive, not a rewrite (the keystone)

Today the geometry layer integrates from a hard-wired origin: `centerline.ts:90-92`
seeds `psi = 0` and `(x,z) = (0,0)`, and `heightAt` measures from the `s=0` datum.
Everything downstream — `planarPoseAt`, `headingAt`, `centerlineAt`, `cantAt`,
`placeOnCentreline` — is integral geometry *relative to that seed*.

The **only** change to make a route into a graph **Edge** is to replace that hard-wired
seed with a per-edge **EdgeFrame**. The arc-accumulation arithmetic in `planarPoseAt`'s
`step()` closure is **byte-identical**; we feed it the local (ψ₀=0) result and apply a
constant rotation by `psi0` plus a translate to `(x0,z0)` and a height datum shift `h0`
*outside* the kernel. Oracle **O5b** (`centerline.test.ts:188`) *already proves* that
"arc 2, integrated from ψ₀=θ₁ and the running (x,z), equals a hand-rotation of arc 2 by
ψ₀ added to arc 1's endpoint." That is **exactly** the edge-join math, one level up: the
parent edge's exit pose **is** the child edge's `EdgeFrame`. The graph join reuses a
proof we already have.

> **Identity special case.** `EdgeFrame = {psi0:0,x0:0,z0:0,h0:0}` reproduces today's
> single route byte-for-byte. The existing route is the graph with one edge and the
> identity frame — so every existing centreline/route/signalling oracle passes unchanged.

### 3.1 Module layering (the honest O17 split)

The panel flagged the winner's O17 story as under-articulated. V1 splits the graph
family so the physics-path import fence is a **true** statement, not a hand-wave:

```
                         imports centerline?   in O17 physics fence?
  src/sim/graph.ts         no (route only)       yes  (types: Edge/Path/TrainPosition…)
  src/sim/graph-geom.ts    YES (centerline)      NO   ← sanctioned spatial consumer
  src/sim/graph-step.ts    no                    yes  (stepOnGraph, occupancy)
  src/sim/ai.ts            no                    yes  (aiInputs — a 1-D controller)
  src/sim/multitrain.ts    no                    yes  (tickAll orchestration)
  src/sim/testbed.ts       via graph-geom        n/a  (scenario DATA; uses exitFrame)
```

- **`graph.ts`** holds only the pure TYPES (`EdgeFrame`, `Edge`, `TrackGraph`,
  `TrainPosition`, `Path`, `IDENTITY_FRAME`) and the trivial `successor(path, edgeId)`
  helper. It imports `route.ts` only — **centerline-free**, so every 1-D module can
  import it without reaching the spatial core.
- **`graph-geom.ts`** is the *only* new module that imports `centerline.ts`: the
  frame-aware geometry wrappers (`planarPoseOnEdge`/`heightOnEdge`/`placeOnEdge`) and
  `exitFrame`/`validateGraph`. It is the **sanctioned spatial-graph consumer** —
  deliberately kept OUT of the O17 physics fence (with a comment in `world.test.ts`).
  The render layer and the camera read geometry through this module.
- **`graph-step.ts`**, **`ai.ts`**, **`multitrain.ts`** are pure 1-D logic over edge-local
  `s` — they never touch the spatial core and are **added to the O17 fenced list**.

This makes the fence assertion honest: *the longitudinal dynamics path (physics,
simulation, controls, aws, train, ai, graph-step, multitrain) never imports
centerline/terrain/camera; only `graph-geom.ts` and the render layer do.*

### 3.2 `EdgeFrame`, `Edge`, types (`src/sim/graph.ts`, centerline-free)

```ts
/** The integration origin for an edge. Identity reproduces today's single route. */
export interface EdgeFrame {
  psi0: number; // entry heading ψ₀ (rad); seeds planarPose's psi
  x0: number;   // entry world X (m)
  z0: number;   // entry world Z (m)
  h0: number;   // entry height datum (m); seeds heightAt's 0
}
export const IDENTITY_FRAME: EdgeFrame = { psi0: 0, x0: 0, z0: 0, h0: 0 };

/** An Edge IS a Route (same 1-D profiles, signals, optional viaducts/tunnels/seed)
 *  read through a frame, plus a stable id. `Route` is reused verbatim. */
export interface Edge { id: string; route: Route; frame: EdgeFrame; }

/** Replaces the scalar SimState.chainage with a position ON THE GRAPH. */
export interface TrainPosition { edgeId: string; s: number; d: number; }

/** A booked route = ordered edge ids, parent→child, continuity-checked (P2). */
export type Path = string[];

/** A bag of edges. Adjacency lives in the Paths (scenario data); no turnouts. */
export interface TrackGraph { edges: Record<string, Edge>; }

export function successor(path: Path, edgeId: string): string | null {
  const i = path.indexOf(edgeId);
  return i >= 0 && i + 1 < path.length ? path[i + 1]! : null;
}
```

### 3.3 Frame-aware geometry (`src/sim/graph-geom.ts`, the sanctioned spatial consumer)

The existing geometry functions are **NOT edited**; these wrappers pre-seed nothing and
post-transform the local pose by the frame:

```ts
// graph-geom.ts — pure; imports centerline.ts + route.ts + graph.ts. NEVER render.
export function planarPoseOnEdge(edge: Edge, s: number)
  : { x: number; z: number; heading: number } {
  const local = planarPoseAt(edge.route, s);            // UNCHANGED core, ψ₀=0 origin
  const { psi0, x0, z0 } = edge.frame;
  const c = Math.cos(psi0), sn = Math.sin(psi0);
  return {
    x: x0 + c * local.x + sn * local.z,                 // rotate local (x,z) by ψ₀ …
    z: z0 - sn * local.x + c * local.z,                  // … then translate to (x0,z0)
    heading: psi0 + local.heading,                       // … and add ψ₀ to the heading
  };
}
export function heightOnEdge(edge: Edge, s: number): number {
  return edge.frame.h0 + heightAt(edge.route, s);        // datum shift only
}
export function placeOnEdge(edge: Edge, s: number, d: number)
  : { x: number; y: number; z: number; heading: number } {
  const p = planarPoseOnEdge(edge, s);
  const rx = Math.cos(p.heading), rz = -Math.sin(p.heading); // same right-normal as core
  return { x: p.x + d * rx, y: heightOnEdge(edge, s), z: p.z + d * rz, heading: p.heading };
}
/** The exit frame of an edge = its pose at s = length, as an EdgeFrame. The value a
 *  child edge's `frame` MUST equal (≤1e-6) for continuity. */
export function exitFrame(edge: Edge): EdgeFrame {
  const p = planarPoseOnEdge(edge, edge.route.length);
  return { psi0: p.heading, x0: p.x, z0: p.z, h0: heightOnEdge(edge, edge.route.length) };
}
```

`cantAt` is **frame-invariant** (depends only on κ and v) and is reused unchanged; only
the *heading* gains `psi0`, applied here at the `placeOnEdge`/`centerlineAt` adapter.

**Continuity, true by construction (`validateGraph`).** The normal authoring path
*derives* each child's `frame` from its parent's `exitFrame`, so continuity holds by
construction; `validateGraph` is the machine check that proves it and catches a
hand-authored mismatch (and, at a **merge**, that *every* parent of a merge edge shares
the merge entry frame):

```ts
// graph-geom.ts — pure. Throws (or returns a non-empty defect list) on any violation.
export function validateGraph(graph: TrackGraph, paths: Path[]): GraphDefect[] {
  const defects: GraphDefect[] = [];
  for (const path of paths)
    for (let i = 0; i + 1 < path.length; i++) {
      const parent = graph.edges[path[i]!], child = graph.edges[path[i + 1]!];
      const want = exitFrame(parent);
      if (!frameClose(child.frame, want, 1e-6))          // (x,z), psi0, h0 all ≤1e-6
        defects.push({ kind: "discontinuity", parent: parent.id, child: child.id });
    }
  // Plus per-edge invariants (P3): minCurveRadius≥250, ribbon≤½R, κ=0 bands, cant≤ceiling.
  return defects;
}
```

### 3.4 `stepOnGraph` — the join wrapper (`src/sim/graph-step.ts`, centerline-free)

The most error-prone seam, so it is the thinnest possible pure layer over the **unchanged**
`step()` and gets the most oracles (R2/R3):

```ts
// graph-step.ts — pure; imports simulation.ts + route.ts + graph.ts (types). NO centerline.
export interface GraphStepResult { state: SimState; pos: TrainPosition; }

export function stepOnGraph(
  graph: TrackGraph, path: Path, spec: TrainSpec,
  state: SimState, pos: TrainPosition, inputs: SimInputs, dt: number,
): GraphStepResult {
  const edge = graph.edges[pos.edgeId]!;
  const next = step(spec, edge.route, state, inputs, dt);   // UNCHANGED core kernel
  const nextEdgeId = successor(path, pos.edgeId);
  if (next.chainage >= edge.route.length && next.speed > 0 && nextEdgeId) {
    const residual = next.chainage - edge.route.length;     // metres past the join
    const handed: SimState = { ...next, chainage: residual };
    return { state: handed, pos: { edgeId: nextEdgeId, s: residual, d: pos.d } };
  }
  // No successor ⇒ the end-clamp speed=0 at route.length (simulation.ts:88-91) SURVIVES:
  // this edge is a true buffer-stop edge (last in the Path).
  return { state: next, pos: { edgeId: pos.edgeId, s: next.chainage, d: pos.d } };
}
```

**Junction orientation/sign reconciliation (D-DEC-1, the named hazard) is a non-problem
by construction.** Every edge in every `Path` is authored **forward** — `s` increases
0→length in the booked direction of travel, and `EdgeFrame` already encodes the world
heading at the join (`exitFrame` gives the child its `psi0`). So at a join the residual is
**always positive `s`** on the next edge, `dir` is **always +1**, and there is **no sign
flip to reconcile**. A train "looped to be passed" runs forward through the loop edge
(curving out and back via its own curvature) and forward through the loop-exit edge.

> **dt safety.** With the harness `dt` clamped to ≤0.05 (MAX_DT) and edges ≫ one step
> long, at most **one** join is crossed per tick, so no multi-edge carry loop is needed.
> (A defensive single re-entry covers a pathological short edge; the testbed never needs it.)

### 3.5 Occupancy — derived clear-block tokens folded into the EXISTING cascade

Occupancy is **never stored**. It is a pure predicate over **all** trains' positions,
computed once per tick and folded into `aspectAt` as a **richer source set**, exactly as
the brief mandates — *positive membership*, not a new primitive and not a station
deletion. A starter's `protects` field may name a **station** (cleared by being served)
*or* a **block id** (an edge id; cleared by being *unoccupied by another train*); the
source set the cascade reads is the union of served stations and **clear** block tokens:

```ts
// graph-step.ts — pure. Block granularity = WHOLE EDGE (D-DEC-3).
/** The edges currently occupied: each train holds its current edge AND reserves the
 *  next booked edge within RESERVE_M of the join. Reservation contention resolves by
 *  lower trainId (deterministic, order-independent; see §3.7). */
export function occupiedEdges(trains: OccInput[]): ReadonlySet<string> { /* current ∪ reserved */ }

/** The source set fed to aspectAt for ONE train: served stations ∪ the block tokens
 *  of every edge NOT occupied by another train. Occupied-by-another ⇒ token ABSENT ⇒
 *  aspectAt's `!source.has(sig.protects)` ⇒ RED. The R→Y→YY→G cascade and its
 *  strictly-increasing-index termination proof are reused VERBATIM. */
export function aspectSource(
  baseServed: ReadonlySet<string>,
  allBlockIds: readonly string[],
  occByOthers: ReadonlySet<string>,
): ReadonlySet<string> {
  const src = new Set(baseServed);
  for (const b of allBlockIds) if (!occByOthers.has(b)) src.add(b); // CLEAR ⇒ present
  return src;
}
```

This is the brief's "player delays AI by occupying a block ahead" as a **richer set
source**: when the player occupies the onward block the looped AI must exit into, that
edge token is **absent** from the AI's `aspectSource`, so the AI's loop-exit starter is
RED, so the AI brakes and holds *in the loop*. When the player clears the block, the
token reappears next tick and the starter returns to YELLOW/GREEN. The latch-release
contract (`controls.ts:241-251`) is **untouched**: occupancy is a *signal aspect* the AI
re-reads each tick, never a latched penalty — a persisting hold simply keeps failing the
"block clear" predicate every tick, which is the contract's "source still demands it"
shape, with no release machine to touch.

### 3.6 `aiInputs` — the AI controller (`src/sim/ai.ts`, centerline-free, fenced)

```ts
// ai.ts — pure; imports route.ts + physics.ts + simulation.ts + graph.ts. NO render,
// NO centerline — a 1-D longitudinal controller, the same layer as controls.ts.
/** Produces the SAME SimInputs resolveInputs produces, so it plugs in at the EXACT
 *  physics seam. Target = MIN(PSR ahead, braking-curve ceiling √(2·a·d) to a red,
 *  line cap); bang-bang with a hysteresis band. */
export function aiInputs(
  edge: Edge, state: SimState, aspectAhead: Aspect,
  distToSignalAhead: number, spec: TrainSpec, mu: number,
): SimInputs {
  const psr = speedLimitAt(edge.route, state.chainage);
  const curveCeil = brakingCurveCeiling(aspectAhead, distToSignalAhead, spec); // v²=2·a·d
  const target = Math.min(psr, curveCeil, AI_LINE_CAP);
  const v = Math.abs(state.speed);
  return {
    notch: v < target - AI_HYST ? 1 : 0,
    brake: v > target + AI_HYST ? 1 : 0,
    dir: 1, mu, emergency: false,                          // dir always +1 (§3.4)
  };
}
```

**Fidelity (D-DEC-4): PSR + braking-curve tracker ONLY** — no dwell/timetable/serving.
`brakingCurveCeiling` reuses the **same** service-brake decel the player's braking obeys
(`physics.ts`), so AI and player share one physics truth. A red ahead drives `curveCeil→0`
as the train nears the post, braking it smoothly to a stand and holding (D-DEC-7: the AI
reads the aspect; it runs no AWS/TPWS/DSD — there is no driver to warn).

### 3.7 The multi-train tick (order-independent) — `src/sim/multitrain.ts`

```ts
// multitrain.ts — pure orchestration; imports graph.ts + graph-step.ts + ai.ts +
// simulation.ts + route.ts. NO render, NO centerline.
export interface TrainRecord {
  id: string; path: Path; pos: TrainPosition; state: SimState; kind: "player" | "ai";
}
/** One tick: derive occupancy from ALL trains' frame-N positions, THEN step every train
 *  on the shared clamped dt. Occupancy is sampled BEFORE any train moves, so the tick is
 *  order-independent (advance-first-then-signal preserved). */
export function tickAll(
  graph: TrackGraph, records: TrainRecord[], dt: number, mu: number, playerInputs: SimInputs,
): TrainRecord[] {
  const occ = occupiedEdges(records.map(toOccInput));          // 1. frame-N snapshot
  return records.map((r) => {                                  // 2. step each on the same dt
    const occByOthers = withoutOwn(occ, r);
    const inputs = r.kind === "player" ? playerInputs
      : aiInputs(graph.edges[r.pos.edgeId]!, r.state, aspectAheadFor(graph, r, occByOthers),
                 distToSignalAhead(graph, r), spec(r), mu);
    return { ...r, ...stepOnGraph(graph, r.path, spec(r), r.state, r.pos, inputs, dt) };
  });
}
```

**Tie-break for block contention (D-DEC-5).** Occupancy is sampled **once, from frame-N
positions, before anyone moves**, so the tick is order-independent. *Physical* occupancy
(a train already on an edge) always counts. *Reservation* contention — two trains both
within `RESERVE_M` of entering the same currently-empty edge in one frame — resolves by
**lower `trainId` proceeds, higher yields**: the higher-id train sees the edge reserved
and is held RED; the lower-id train enters. This is deterministic and order-independent
(it depends only on positions and stable ids, never iteration order), and — unlike bare
mutual-yield — **cannot wedge**: exactly one train advances. No first-come arbitration, no
sequence number; a stable total order on a key the records already carry.

### 3.8 Render — AI trains as world meshes (`src/render/scene.ts`, impure)

AI trains are **new meshes** placed with the same primitive lineside furniture uses:
`placeOnEdge(edge, s, d)` → `(x,y,z,heading)` (from `graph-geom.ts`, the sanctioned
spatial consumer; sim→render is allowed). The render layer maps each AI `TrainRecord` to a
box/EMU mesh and sets its transform; the player's camera stays single-train. The only new
render concept is `placeOnCentreline` generalised to `placeOnEdge` — the same
generalisation the geometry layer received.

### 3.9 Wiring summary (one paragraph, one head)

The impure shell (`main.ts`) builds the `TrackGraph` and the `Path`s from `testbed.ts`,
runs `validateGraph` once at startup (fail-fast on a malformed scenario), holds an array of
`TrainRecord`s, and each frame: reads the wall clock once for `dt` (the only clock), runs
the player's existing keyboard→`reduceControls`→`resolveInputs` pipeline to get
`playerInputs`, calls the **pure** `tickAll(graph, records, dt, mu, playerInputs)`, runs the
player's existing `tickAws`/`tickSafety` on the player record (edge-local `s`), then renders
every record via `placeOnEdge` and updates the HUD from the player record. **All logic is
pure and tested; the shell has none.**

## 5. Acceptance criteria — numbered pure oracles (definition of done)

All oracles are pure (no DOM, no Three, no wall clock), driven by explicit `dt` and
positions. "≈" means `toBeCloseTo` at the stated decimals.

**Frame & geometry**

- **P0 (identity).** For the legacy route as an edge with `IDENTITY_FRAME`,
  `planarPoseOnEdge`/`heightOnEdge`/`placeOnEdge` ≈ `planarPoseAt`/`heightAt`/
  `placeOnCentreline` at **12 dp** for `s ∈ {0,1000,6000,…}`, `d ∈ {−3,0,7}`.
- **P1 (composition = O5b lifted).** Build edge B with `frame = exitFrame(A)` for two
  arcs A,B. `planarPoseOnEdge(B, sB)` ≈ the hand-rotation of B's local pose by A's exit
  heading added to A's exit point, at **6 dp** — the graph join reproduces O5b's two-arc
  result with B as a separate edge.
- **P2 (continuity, by construction).** `validateGraph` returns **no defects** for the
  testbed: for every parent→child in every Path, `entryFrame(child) == exitFrame(parent)`
  in (x,z), `psi0`, and `h0` within **1e-6**; at the merge edge, *both* loop and main
  parents share its entry frame.
- **P2-NEG (the check has teeth).** Perturb one child `frame` by `1e-3`; `validateGraph`
  returns a `discontinuity` defect for exactly that pair.
- **P3 (per-edge invariants).** For every edge: `minCurveRadius(edge.route) ≥ 250`; if
  terrain-rendered, `ribbonHalfWidth ≤ 0.5·minCurveRadius`; every viaduct/tunnel band lies
  wholly on a κ=0 segment; every curve's cant stays off the ceiling (`0.08·|κ|·v² ≤ 0.105`).

**Join (`stepOnGraph`) — headline differential first**

- **G-DIFF (differential carry-over — the primary join proof).** Build edges A and
  B=`exitFrame(A)`, and a SINGLE synthetic `Route` whose curvature/grade/speed segments
  are A's then B's laid end-to-end (B offset by `A.length`). Drive a train from `s=0`
  across the A→B boundary on the **graph** (`stepOnGraph`) and along the **concatenated
  route** (plain `step()`) with an *identical* input sequence; assert the world pose
  (`placeOnEdge` vs `placeOnCentreline`) **and** cumulative along-path distance agree to
  **1e-9** at every step. Pins the residual arithmetic against an independent construction.
- **O-STEP-PARITY (wrapper is a no-op on the legacy case).** On a single-edge
  `IDENTITY_FRAME` graph with no successor, `stepOnGraph` returns **bit-identical** state
  to a direct `step()` for a fixed input sequence.
- **J1 (residual carry).** A train with `speed>0` whose `step()` would put `chainage` at
  `edge.length + r` lands at `edgeId = successor`, `s ≈ r`, with `speed`/`brakeActual`/
  `time` carried unchanged.
- **J2 (world continuity across the join).** `placeOnEdge(parent, parent.length, d)` ≈
  `placeOnEdge(child, 0, d)` at **1e-6** for the booked pair *(follows from P2)*.
- **J3 (buffer-stop survival).** On the **last** edge of a Path (no successor), the
  end-clamp `speed=0 at route.length` (`simulation.ts:88-91`) still fires.

**Occupancy & AI**

- **OCC1.** `occupiedEdges` returns each train's current edge, and its next booked edge
  once `edgeLen − s ≤ RESERVE_M`.
- **OCC2 (richer-source RED).** With the player occupying the shared onward block `M`,
  `aspectSource` for the looped AI **omits** `M`'s token, so `aspectAt` for the AI's
  loop-exit starter is **RED**; the R→Y→YY→G cascade behind it is unchanged.
- **OCC3 (release).** When the player leaves `M`, its token reappears in `aspectSource` and
  the starter returns to YELLOW/GREEN. *(No latch touched — pure per-tick re-derivation.)*
- **AI1 (tracks PSR).** On a clear edge with posted limit `v`, `aiInputs` drives the train
  to `≈ v` (within the hysteresis band) and holds.
- **AI2 (brakes to a red).** With `aspectAhead = RED` at distance `d`, integrating
  `stepOnGraph` forward brings the train to a **stand at or before the post**
  (final `s ≤ postS`, `speed ≈ 0`).
- **AI3 (proceeds on clear).** From the held stand, flip `aspectAhead` to GREEN; `speed`
  strictly increases over the next ticks.

**Multi-train & the headline scenarios**

- **MT1 (order independence).** `tickAll` over `records` in **any permutation** yields
  identical post-tick `(edgeId,s,speed)` for every train.
- **MT2 (deterministic contention).** Two trains booked to enter one currently-empty edge
  in the same frame: the **lower-id** train enters; the **higher-id** train is held RED and
  yields (no wedge, no priority field — a total order on `trainId`).
- **S1 (DONE — player delays AND overtakes a looped AI).** On the testbed graph, the player
  runs the **through main** while an AI service is booked via the **passing loop**. The AI
  reaches its loop-exit starter, which is held **RED** because the player occupies the
  shared onward block `E_main_out`; the AI **brakes to a stand and waits in the loop**
  (oracle: AI `speed` reaches 0 and stays 0 while the player occupies `E_main_out`). The
  player **passes** and clears `E_main_out`; the AI's starter clears and the AI **resumes
  and completes its Path** to its buffer-stop edge (oracle: AI `speed>0` resumes within K
  ticks; final `pos.edgeId` is the last edge with `speed ≈ 0` at the buffers). Fully
  deterministic, dt-driven.
- **S2 (branch divergence).** A second AI booked `[E_main_in, E_branch, E_branch_buffer]`
  diverges at the throat and runs to its buffer; `validateGraph` passes for the branch
  Path, and the branch AI's world track visibly departs the main (P1/J2 continuity on the
  divergent frame). Exercises the alternate route and, with a staggered start sharing
  `E_main_in`, the MT2 tie-break.

## 6. The concrete first testbed graph (`src/sim/testbed.ts`)

A minimal graph exhibiting all three required features — multiple tracks, a junction/points,
an alternate route — frames threaded by `exitFrame`:

```
                              E_loop  (curves out & back, rejoins)
                            ┌───────────────────────────┐
  E_main_in ──[throat J1]──┤                            ├──[merge J2]── E_main_out ── E_main_buffer
   (IDENTITY)               └───── E_main_through ───────┘                (=exitFrame
        │                         (straight main)                          E_main_through
        └──── E_branch ──── E_branch_buffer                                 = exitFrame E_loop)
              (diverges)      (buffer stop)
```

- **E_main_in** — straight approach, ~800 m, `IDENTITY_FRAME`. Ends at throat **J1**.
- **E_main_through** — the straight through main between the points and the merge, ~400 m,
  `frame = exitFrame(E_main_in)`. The **player** runs this.
- **E_loop** — the passing-loop road, ~400 m, gentle out-and-back curvature
  (`|κ| ≤ 1/300`, `R ≥ 300 ≥ 250`), `frame = exitFrame(E_main_in)`. Authored so its **exit
  frame equals `E_main_through`'s exit frame** (net-zero heading change, same merge point)
  — the merge-continuity constraint P2 enforces. Carries the loop-exit starter protecting
  block `E_main_out`.
- **E_main_out** — the through continuation past the merge **J2**, ~800 m, `frame =
  exitFrame(E_main_through) = exitFrame(E_loop)`. The shared onward block the player
  occupies to hold the looped AI. Ends at **E_main_buffer** (buffer stop).
- **E_branch** — the diverging branch, ~600 m, opening curve then straight, `frame =
  exitFrame(E_main_in)` with a different `psi0`/curvature (the divergent road). Ends at
  **E_branch_buffer** (buffer stop).

**Paths (scenario DATA):**

- **Player** (through main, does the overtaking): `[E_main_in, E_main_through, E_main_out, E_main_buffer]`.
- **AI-1** (looped, delayed & overtaken): `[E_main_in, E_loop, E_main_out, E_main_buffer]`.
- **AI-2** (branch divergence): `[E_main_in, E_branch, E_branch_buffer]`.

This is the smallest graph with a real passing loop (diverge + converge), a branch (the
alternate route), and exactly the occupancy interaction S1 requires.

## 7. Decision log (resolves all 8 open decisions)

**D-DEC-1 — Junction orientation/sign reconciliation.** *Resolved: all-forward authoring →
carry the residual, keep `dir=+1`; no sign flip.* Reverse running does not cross joins
(deferred non-goal). Oracles: **G-DIFF** (differential), J1/J2, G-class dt-slice stability.
*Rationale: the simplest provably-continuous model; cutting reverse-through-joins removes
the entire reconciliation surface (the panel-rejected `reversed[]` machinery).*

**D-DEC-2 — `stepOnGraph` as a NEW layer vs thickening `step()`.** *Resolved: NEW thin pure
wrapper; `step()` byte-identical.* Oracles: **O-STEP-PARITY**, G-DIFF. *Rationale: protect
the asset; the seam owns its own oracles without endangering the proven kernel.*

**D-DEC-3 — Block granularity & signal kind.** *Resolved: WHOLE-EDGE blocks; OVERLAY the
existing starter cascade as **clear-block tokens** in the `aspectAt` source set — no new
signal kind, no station deletion.* A `protects` field names a station or a block id
uniformly; CLEAR ⇒ token present ⇒ may show non-RED; occupied-by-another ⇒ token absent ⇒
RED. *Rationale: the brief's "richer SET SOURCE not a new primitive", with positive
membership (the panel's preferred mapping over deleting a station).*

**D-DEC-4 — AI fidelity.** *Resolved: PSR + braking-curve tracker ONLY; no dwell/timetable.*
*Rationale: exactly enough to be delayed via occupancy; dwell is a deferred additive Path
annotation feeding the same target — no on-ramp to nowhere.*

**D-DEC-5 — Multi-train tie-break.** *Resolved: occupancy sampled once from frame-N
positions (order-independent); reservation contention resolves by **lower `trainId`
proceeds, higher yields**.* *Rationale: order-independence is a hard constraint; the
lower-id rule is deterministic, adds no happy-path state, and closes the mutual-yield
wedge two judges flagged.*

**D-DEC-6 — Node geometry.** *Resolved: pure frame hand-off; divergence baked into the
branch child edge's opening curvature; merge continuity enforced by `validateGraph`.* A node
stores nothing — it is the equality `entry(child) == exit(parent)`. *Rationale: no turnout
mesh, no blade state; divergence is just an edge that curves, covered by the per-edge
`minCurveRadius≥250` oracle.*

**D-DEC-7 — AI signalling.** *Resolved: reduced — AI reads the aspect and obeys a braking
curve; NO AWS/TPWS/DSD.* *Rationale: the full machine protects a human driver; the AI's
safety is the braking-curve-to-red inside `aiInputs`. Keeps the Phase-2 penalty/latch
machine player-only and untouched.*

**D-DEC-8 — `TrainPosition.d`.** *Resolved: parallel running is TWO DISTINCT EDGES; `d` is a
constant per-train render offset only.* *Rationale: occupancy, signalling, and joins all key
off `edgeId`; one concept per axis — `edgeId` routes, `s` advances, `d` only nudges the mesh.
The passing loop is therefore a real separate edge, which is exactly what makes S1 work.*

## 8. Residual risks

1. **Join correctness depends on all-forward authoring.** A future scenario booking an edge
   *against* its `s` direction breaks the residual-carry assumption. *Mitigation:* P2 +
   G-DIFF catch any frame discontinuity; `validateGraph` is the guard. Reverse-through-joins
   is deferred precisely to keep this true.
2. **Whole-edge occupancy is coarse.** A long edge occupied far from the join still holds a
   follower out. *Mitigation:* acceptable for the testbed (edges sized to the conflict);
   sub-interval blocks are a clean later extension (split an edge into block-sized child
   edges — *more data, no new concept*).
3. **AI bang-bang may chatter** near the target / braking-curve knee. *Mitigation:* the
   hysteresis band; a single-pole filter on the target is a contained follow-up.
   Determinism unaffected (no clock).
4. **Merge geometry is demanding.** The loop must return to the through edge's exit point
   *and* heading. *Mitigation:* P2 enforces it numerically; the author solves the loop
   curvature (symmetric out-and-back) — a one-time authoring cost, machine-checked.
5. **Render perf with N AI meshes** is outside the tested core; the increment ships **two**
   AI trains, so this is a non-issue now, bounded later by mesh reuse.

### Appendix — the seven new pure concepts, in one breath

`EdgeFrame` (un-hardwire the integration origin) → `Edge` (a Route read through a frame) →
`TrackGraph` (a bag of edges joined by `exit==entry`, checked by `validateGraph`) →
`TrainPosition {edgeId,s,d}` (where a train is) → `Path` (the booked edge list) → derived
`occupancy` (clear-block tokens folded into the unchanged `aspectAt`) → `aiInputs` (a
target-speed tracker emitting `SimInputs`) → `stepOnGraph` (carry the residual across a join
over the unchanged `step()`). Nothing else. The asset — `step()`, `acceleration`, the
AWS/TPWS/DSD machine, the latch-release contract, every existing oracle — is reused verbatim.
