# HLD — Track Graph & Reactive AI (V2)

Status: DRAFT (V2 — adversarial loop CONVERGED: round 1 fixed the keystone, round 2 clean at 0
criticals; round-2 polish applied) · 2026.06.20 · additive over the Phase-1/2 core
Repo: `mdtrain2` ("The Night Cab") · Trunk: `origin/master` · Version on implement: **0.12.0 → 0.13.0**

> **Thesis.** A `TrackGraph` is the existing single-route geometry with the integration origin
> un-hardwired: one new field `EdgeFrame{psi0,x0,z0,h0}` turns today's `route` into an `Edge`, and
> the *already-tested* arc-compounding "the next curvature segment starts from the running ψ₀ and
> accumulated (x,z)" (oracle **O5b**) is lifted to "the next **edge** starts from its parent's exit
> frame". Trains carry `{edgeId,s,d}` instead of a scalar chainage; a thin pure `stepOnGraph` wraps
> `step()` to carry the residual across a join. The **only** change to the dynamics kernel is one
> additive, default-preserving parameter (`clampAtEnd`) that lets the wrapper suppress the *terminal*
> end-stop on a non-buffer edge — every existing call site is behaviorally byte-identical. Occupancy
> is a *derived physical set* fed into the *existing* `aspectAt` cascade as namespaced clear-block
> tokens — no new signal primitive. AI is a pure `aiInputs(...)` producing the same `SimInputs` the
> player's `resolveInputs` produces. Seven new pure concepts; no interlocking UI, no pathfinding.

**corrected after adversarial review round 1**, which found one fatal flaw and a simplification cluster.

**The fatal flaw round 1 caught (5 lenses + the devil's advocate converged).** V1's keystone —
"`stepOnGraph` wraps the **byte-identical** `step()` to carry the residual" — is self-contradictory.
The end-clamp in `step()` (`simulation.ts:88-91`) sets `chainage=route.length; speed=0` *inside* the
substep loop the instant `chainage>=route.length && speed>0`. So `step()` never returns
`chainage>length` with `speed>0`; V1's carry guard `next.speed>0` is never true at a join; the
residual is always 0; **every train dead-stops at the end of its first edge** and S1/S2/G-DIFF/J1/AI2
are unreachable. The "zero tested lines changed / byte-identical `step()`" selling point is exactly
what had to give.

**The corrected fix (grounded in the real `simulation.ts`).** Gate **only** the forward end-clamp
behind one additive parameter, `step(spec, route, state, inputs, dt, clampAtEnd = true)`. `route.length`
is used *nowhere else* in `step()`, so the change is surgical: the `else if` at line 88 becomes
`else if (clampAtEnd && chainage >= route.length && speed > 0)`. Every existing caller omits the
argument ⇒ `clampAtEnd=true` ⇒ **behaviorally byte-identical**, every existing oracle passes. Only
`stepOnGraph` passes `clampAtEnd=false`, and only on a **non-terminal** edge, so chainage runs past
`length` with speed retained and the residual is real; on the **terminal** (buffer-stop) edge it uses
the default `true`, so the end-stop (J3) survives. This is the narrowest honest change; the review
panel explicitly endorsed it over the literal-unchanged framing.

## 1. Scope & non-goals

### 1.1 In scope (the first acceptance-tested increment)

A **testbed graph** with a through main, a **passing loop**, and a **junction to a branch**, carrying
deterministic trains:

- the **PLAYER**, driving a booked `Path` (an ordered list of edge ids) the scenario fixes — runs the
  through main and **overtakes** an AI that is looped; **no manual point-setting, no interlocking UI**;
- one **AI service** booked via the **passing loop**, driven by a pure target-speed tracker, that the
  player **delays by physically occupying the shared onward block** (the player on the main occupies
  the block the looped AI must exit into ⇒ the AI's loop-exit signal goes RED ⇒ the AI brakes to a
  stand and waits *in the loop* until the player passes and clears it);
- a **second AI service** booked onto the **branch**, demonstrating divergence.

Delivers: the pure types (`Edge`/`EdgeFrame`, `TrackGraph`, `TrainPosition`, `Path`), the frame-aware
geometry wrappers + `validateGraph`, the derived **physical** occupancy folded into `aspectAt` as
namespaced clear-block tokens, the pure `aiInputs(...)`, the pure `stepOnGraph(...)` over a
`clampAtEnd`-parameterised `step()`, an order-independent multi-train tick, and AI trains rendered as
world meshes via the existing `placeOnEdge` primitive.

### 1.2 Non-goals (deferred — explicitly NOT in this increment)

- **No change to `step()`'s dynamics.** The *only* edit is the additive optional `clampAtEnd` parameter
  (default `true`), which is behaviorally byte-identical at every existing call site; the accel/brake/
  integration arithmetic is untouched.
- **No interlocking UI, no signaller, no runtime pathfinding.** Routes are DATA; points are baked into
  the scenario `Path`s.
- **No reverse running across a junction.** A reversing player runs `s` back down the *current* edge
  and clamps at `s=0` (the start-clamp, unaffected by `clampAtEnd`); `dir=−1` never crosses an edge
  join. (The rejected `reversed[]` surface — designed out, not built.)
- **No reservation / look-ahead occupancy footprint, no tie-break.** Occupancy is **physical**: a train
  occupies its *current* edge only. The (measure-zero) same-frame double-entry of one empty edge at a
  merge is avoided by scenario authoring (staggered starts); reservation is the deferred fix if ever
  needed — "more data, no new concept".
- **No timetable / platform dwell / station-stop for AI.** The AI tracks a speed target and obeys
  signals; a looped AI holds at its loop-exit red until the onward block clears (D-DEC-4).
- **No cross-edge signal look-ahead.** `aspectAt` recurses within one edge's `signals` array; every
  signal an AI must brake for in this increment lies on the AI's **current** edge (true for the
  testbed). Multi-rung caution spanning an edge boundary is a deferred non-goal.
- **No cross-join player AWS continuity.** The player's `tickAws`/`tickSafety` run against the player
  record's **current** edge route with edge-local `(prevS, nowS)`; a frame that crosses a join yields an
  **empty AWS sweep** (the new edge's `prevS` is the small residual, so `nowS ≤ prevS` is vacuous). This
  is harmless because the testbed's player edges carry no stations/signals; AWS-magnet continuity across
  a join is a deferred non-goal alongside cross-edge look-ahead (see §3.9).
- **No `d` as a routing degree of freedom.** `TrainPosition.d` is a constant render offset only;
  parallel running is two distinct edges (D-DEC-8).
- **No turnout geometry / moving-blade meshes.** A node is a pure frame hand-off; divergence is baked
  into the branch child edge's curvature (D-DEC-6).
- **No new safety primitive for AI** (no AWS/TPWS/DSD); the player keeps the full Phase-2 machine
  unchanged (D-DEC-7).
- **No multi-edge occupancy / train-length sweep.** Whole-edge blocks; a train's body is a point at
  `s` for occupancy.

## 3. The design

### 3.0 Why this is additive, not a rewrite (the keystone)

Today the geometry layer integrates from a hard-wired origin: `centerline.ts:~90-92` seeds `psi=0`,
`(x,z)=(0,0)`, and `heightAt` measures from the `s=0` datum. The **only** change to make a route an
`Edge` is to replace that seed with a per-edge **EdgeFrame**, applied as a *rigid post-transform*
outside the kernel. Oracle **O5b** (`centerline.test.ts:188`) already proves "arc 2, integrated from
ψ₀=θ₁ and the running (x,z), equals a hand-rotation of arc 2 by ψ₀ added to arc 1's endpoint" — the
edge-join math one level up. `EdgeFrame = IDENTITY` reproduces today byte-for-byte; the existing route
is the one-edge identity graph, so every existing geometry/route/signalling oracle passes unchanged.

The dynamics kernel `step()` keeps its arithmetic; it gains **one** default-preserving parameter so a
non-terminal edge can defer its end-stop to the next edge (§3.4). That single parameter is the entire
cost of the join on the asset side.

### 3.1 Module layering (the honest, enforced O17 fence)

```
                       imports centerline?   in O17 physics fence?
  src/sim/graph.ts        no (route only)       yes   (types: Edge/Path/TrainPosition/…)
  src/sim/graph-geom.ts   YES (centerline)      NO    ← sanctioned spatial consumer
  src/sim/graph-sim.ts    no                    yes   (stepOnGraph, occupancy, aiInputs, tickAll)
  src/sim/testbed.ts      via graph-geom        n/a   (scenario DATA; uses exitFrame/validateGraph)
```

- **`graph.ts`** — pure TYPES + `successor`. Imports `route.ts` only — centerline-free; fenced.
- **`graph-geom.ts`** — the only new module importing `centerline.ts`: `planarPoseOnEdge`/
  `heightOnEdge`/`placeOnEdge`/`exitFrame`/`validateGraph`. The **sanctioned spatial-graph consumer**,
  deliberately OUT of the O17 fence; render + camera read geometry through it.
- **`graph-sim.ts`** — all the centerline-free 1-D logic: `stepOnGraph`, `occupiedEdges`,
  `aspectSource`, `aiInputs`, `brakingCurveCeiling`, `TrainRecord`, `tickAll`. Fenced. **Must not
  import `graph-geom`** (it needs no geometry).
- The O17 fence (in `world.test.ts`) is extended two ways: (1) add `graph.ts` and `graph-sim.ts` to the
  fenced module list (count `5 → 7`); (2) add `graph-geom` to the denied-basename set *for the fenced
  modules* so a fenced module importing it fails the build — closing the transitive leak and making the
  "the longitudinal path never reaches the spatial core" statement *enforced*, not merely asserted.
  `graph-geom.ts` itself stays out of the globbed fenced set.

### 3.2 `EdgeFrame`, `Edge`, types (`src/sim/graph.ts`, centerline-free)

```ts
export interface EdgeFrame { psi0: number; x0: number; z0: number; h0: number; }
export const IDENTITY_FRAME: EdgeFrame = { psi0: 0, x0: 0, z0: 0, h0: 0 };
export interface Edge { id: string; route: Route; frame: EdgeFrame; } // Route reused verbatim
export interface TrainPosition { edgeId: string; s: number; d: number; }
export type Path = string[];                       // ordered edge ids, parent→child (P2-checked)
export interface TrackGraph { edges: Record<string, Edge>; } // adjacency lives in the Paths
export function successor(path: Path, edgeId: string): string | null {
  const i = path.indexOf(edgeId);
  return i >= 0 && i + 1 < path.length ? path[i + 1]! : null;
}
```

### 3.3 Frame-aware geometry + validation (`src/sim/graph-geom.ts`, sanctioned spatial consumer)

The existing geometry functions are **NOT edited**; these wrappers post-transform the local pose:

```ts
// graph-geom.ts — pure; imports centerline.ts + route.ts + graph.ts. NEVER render, NEVER graph-sim.
export function planarPoseOnEdge(edge: Edge, s: number): { x: number; z: number; heading: number } {
  const local = planarPoseAt(edge.route, s);              // UNCHANGED core, ψ₀=0 origin
  const { psi0, x0, z0 } = edge.frame, c = Math.cos(psi0), sn = Math.sin(psi0);
  return { x: x0 + c * local.x + sn * local.z,
           z: z0 - sn * local.x + c * local.z,
           heading: psi0 + local.heading };
}
export function heightOnEdge(edge: Edge, s: number): number { return edge.frame.h0 + heightAt(edge.route, s); }
export function placeOnEdge(edge: Edge, s: number, d: number): { x: number; y: number; z: number; heading: number } {
  const p = planarPoseOnEdge(edge, s), rx = Math.cos(p.heading), rz = -Math.sin(p.heading);
  return { x: p.x + d * rx, y: heightOnEdge(edge, s), z: p.z + d * rz, heading: p.heading };
}
export function exitFrame(edge: Edge): EdgeFrame {
  const p = planarPoseOnEdge(edge, edge.route.length);
  return { psi0: p.heading, x0: p.x, z0: p.z, h0: heightOnEdge(edge, edge.route.length) };
}
```

`cantAt` is frame-invariant (κ, v only) and reused unchanged; only the heading gains `psi0`.

**`validateGraph` — continuity true by construction, plus the new structural invariants:**

```ts
// graph-geom.ts — pure. Returns a (possibly empty) defect list; main.ts fails fast at startup.
export function validateGraph(graph: TrackGraph, paths: Path[], stations: ReadonlySet<string>,
                              maxSpeed: number): GraphDefect[] {
  // For each parent→child in each Path: entryFrame(child) == exitFrame(parent) within 1e-6 in
  //   (x,z), psi0, h0 — at a MERGE, every parent of a merge edge must share its entry frame (P2).
  // P-LEN:  every edge.route.length > maxSpeed * MAX_DT * SAFETY (one join per tick, machine-enforced).
  // OCC-NS: the station-id set and the edge-id set are DISJOINT (block tokens never alias stations).
  // NO-REPEAT: no Path repeats an edge id (successor()'s indexOf-first-match assumption holds).
  // P3:     per-edge minCurveRadius≥250 / ribbon≤½R / κ=0 bands / cant≤ceiling, over graph.edges.
}
```

The normal authoring path *derives* `child.frame = exitFrame(parent)`, so continuity holds by
construction; `validateGraph` proves it and catches any hand-authored mismatch (P2/P2-NEG). A node
stores nothing — it is the equality `entry(child) == exit(parent)`.

### 3.4 `stepOnGraph` — the join wrapper (`src/sim/graph-sim.ts`, centerline-free)

The minimal change to the asset is the `clampAtEnd` parameter; the wrapper is otherwise thin:

```ts
// step() gains ONE optional default-preserving param (simulation.ts):
//   export function step(spec, route, state, inputs, dt, clampAtEnd = true): SimState
//   …and line 88 becomes:  } else if (clampAtEnd && chainage >= route.length && speed > 0) {
// Every existing caller omits the arg ⇒ clampAtEnd=true ⇒ behaviorally byte-identical.

// graph-sim.ts — pure; imports simulation.ts + physics.ts + route.ts + graph.ts. NO centerline.
export interface GraphStepResult { state: SimState; pos: TrainPosition; }
export function stepOnGraph(graph: TrackGraph, path: Path, spec: TrainSpec,
                            state: SimState, pos: TrainPosition, inputs: SimInputs, dt: number): GraphStepResult {
  const edge = graph.edges[pos.edgeId]!;
  const nextEdgeId = successor(path, pos.edgeId);
  const isTerminal = nextEdgeId === null;
  const next = step(spec, edge.route, state, inputs, dt, /* clampAtEnd */ isTerminal);
  if (!isTerminal && next.chainage >= edge.route.length && next.speed > 0) {
    const residual = next.chainage - edge.route.length;        // > 0 because the clamp was suppressed
    const handed: SimState = { ...next, chainage: residual };   // speed/brakeActual/time carry
    return { state: handed, pos: { edgeId: nextEdgeId!, s: residual, d: pos.d } };
  }
  // Terminal edge ⇒ clampAtEnd was true ⇒ end-stop fired (J3). Non-crossing ⇒ stayed on this edge.
  return { state: next, pos: { edgeId: pos.edgeId, s: next.chainage, d: pos.d } };
}
```

**Junction orientation/sign reconciliation (D-DEC-1) is a non-problem by construction.** Every edge in
every `Path` is authored forward (`s` increases in the booked direction); `EdgeFrame` carries the
world heading at the join, so the residual is always positive `s` on the next edge and `dir` is always
`+1` — no sign flip. Reverse running does not cross joins (the start-clamp at `simulation.ts:85-87` is
unaffected by `clampAtEnd` and still clamps a reversing train at `s=0`). One join per tick is
*machine-enforced* by `validateGraph`'s P-LEN invariant (residual ≤ `maxSpeed·MAX_DT` ≪ min edge
length), so no carry loop is needed.

> The ≤~one-tick residual integrated just past a join uses the parent edge's end conditions (route
> lookups clamp to the last band) rather than the child's — a sub-metre, sub-tick approximation that is
> physically negligible. G-DIFF (§5) isolates the *carry arithmetic* from this by using constant-grade
> test edges, where parent-end and child-start forcing are identical.

### 3.5 Occupancy — derived PHYSICAL clear-block tokens, folded into the EXISTING cascade

Occupancy is **never stored** and is **physical only**: a train occupies its *current* edge. It is a
pure predicate over all trains' positions, computed once per tick and folded into `aspectAt` as a
richer source Set — *positive membership* of **namespaced** clear-block tokens (a block token is an
edge id; `validateGraph`'s OCC-NS invariant guarantees edge-ids never alias station-ids):

```ts
// graph-sim.ts — pure. WHOLE-EDGE blocks, physical occupancy (D-DEC-3, D-DEC-5).
export function occupiedEdges(records: { id: string; pos: TrainPosition }[]): ReadonlySet<string> {
  return new Set(records.map((r) => r.pos.edgeId));        // each train's CURRENT edge only
}
/** The source set fed to aspectAt for ONE train: served stations ∪ the tokens of every BLOCK edge
 *  NOT occupied by another train. Occupied-by-another ⇒ token ABSENT ⇒ aspectAt's
 *  `!source.has(sig.protects)` ⇒ RED. The R→Y→YY→G cascade + its termination proof are VERBATIM. */
export function aspectSource(baseServed: ReadonlySet<string>, blockEdgeIds: readonly string[],
                            occByOthers: ReadonlySet<string>): ReadonlySet<string> {
  const src = new Set(baseServed);
  for (const e of blockEdgeIds) if (!occByOthers.has(e)) src.add(e);  // CLEAR ⇒ token present
  return src;
}
```

"Player delays AI by occupying a block ahead" is then a richer set source: a loop-exit starter on
`E_loop` has `protects = "E_main_out"`; while the player physically occupies `E_main_out`, that token
is absent from the AI's `aspectSource`, so the starter is RED and the AI holds *in the loop*; when the
player clears `E_main_out` the token reappears next tick and the starter clears. The latch-release
contract (`controls.ts:241-251`) is **untouched** — occupancy is a *signal aspect* re-read each tick,
never a latched penalty; a persisting hold simply keeps failing the "block clear" predicate, which is
the contract's "source still demands it" shape, with no release machine to touch.

> Block-protecting starters live on the AI's **current** edge and participate in the **same**
> within-edge R→Y→YY→G cascade (a red block signal cautions the signal behind it — correct UK
> behaviour). Cross-edge caution look-ahead is a deferred non-goal (§1.2).

### 3.6 `aiInputs` — the AI controller (`src/sim/graph-sim.ts`, centerline-free, fenced)

```ts
/** Produces the SAME SimInputs resolveInputs produces, at the EXACT physics seam. Target = MIN(PSR
 *  ahead, braking-curve ceiling √(2·a·d) to a red on THIS edge, line cap); bang-bang + hysteresis. */
export function aiInputs(edge: Edge, state: SimState, aspectAhead: Aspect, distToSignalAhead: number,
                        spec: TrainSpec, mu: number): SimInputs {
  const psr = speedLimitAt(edge.route, state.chainage);
  const curveCeil = brakingCurveCeiling(aspectAhead, distToSignalAhead, spec);  // v²=2·a·d
  const target = Math.min(psr, curveCeil, AI_LINE_CAP), v = Math.abs(state.speed);
  return { notch: v < target - AI_HYST ? 1 : 0, brake: v > target + AI_HYST ? 1 : 0,
           dir: 1, mu, emergency: false };
}
```

PSR + braking-curve tracker only — no dwell/timetable (D-DEC-4); reads the aspect, runs no AWS/TPWS/DSD
(D-DEC-7). The ceiling brakes **only for RED** (per AI2), using the train's service-brake decel
(`spec.brakeServiceDecel`, a `TrainSpec` field — the same decel the player's braking obeys), so AI and
player share one physics truth:
`brakingCurveCeiling = aspectAhead === "RED" ? Math.sqrt(2 * spec.brakeServiceDecel * distToSignalAhead) : Infinity`.
The aspect/distance the AI brakes for are computed by `tickAll` from the AI's **current** edge `signals`
(cross-edge look-ahead is a non-goal); both are pinned by AI2.

### 3.7 The multi-train tick (order-independent) — `src/sim/graph-sim.ts`

```ts
export interface TrainRecord { id: string; path: Path; pos: TrainPosition; state: SimState; kind: "player" | "ai"; }
/** Occupancy/signals are sampled ONCE from frame-N (pre-move) positions, then every train advances on
 *  the shared clamped dt. The single pre-move snapshot is what makes the tick permutation-invariant
 *  (MT1) — not an advance/signal ordering. */
export function tickAll(graph: TrackGraph, records: TrainRecord[], blockEdgeIds: readonly string[],
                       dt: number, mu: number, playerInputs: SimInputs): TrainRecord[] {
  const occ = occupiedEdges(records);                                  // ONE frame-N snapshot
  return records.map((r) => {
    const occByOthers = new Set([...occ].filter((e) => e !== r.pos.edgeId)); // others, from the snapshot
    const inputs = r.kind === "player" ? playerInputs
      : aiInputs(graph.edges[r.pos.edgeId]!, r.state,
                 aspectAheadFor(graph, r, blockEdgeIds, occByOthers),
                 distToSignalAhead(graph, r), spec(r), mu);
    return { ...r, ...stepOnGraph(graph, r.path, spec(r), r.state, r.pos, inputs, dt) };
  });
}
// The AI glue helpers are small functions over already-verified primitives. NOTE the real signature
// nextSignalAhead(route, s, dir): {i, sig} | null  (route.ts:260-271 — dir is REQUIRED; null for dir=-1
// or no signal ahead; the post chainage is n.sig.chainage and its index is n.i). The AI is always
// dir=+1, serves no stations (base served = EMPTY), and has no signal ahead ⇒ unbounded distance/GREEN:
//   distToSignalAhead(graph,r): const n = nextSignalAhead(edge.route, r.state.chainage, 1);
//                               return n ? n.sig.chainage - r.state.chainage : Infinity;
//   aspectAheadFor(graph,r,blockEdgeIds,occByOthers):
//                               const n = nextSignalAhead(edge.route, r.state.chainage, 1);
//                               return n ? aspectAt(edge.route, n.i,
//                                                   aspectSource(EMPTY, blockEdgeIds, occByOthers)) : "GREEN";
//   spec(r) = the record's TrainSpec.  All read the AI's CURRENT edge only (cross-edge non-goal).
```

**No tie-break, no priority field, no reservation.** With physical-only occupancy a follower simply
sees the leader's edge RED and brakes — ordinary block signalling; there is no same-frame reservation
race to arbitrate. MT1 (permutation invariance) is the real hard constraint and holds because
occupancy is a single frame-N snapshot of *current* edges and set operations are order-independent. The
only same-frame coincidence physical occupancy does not resolve — two trains entering one empty edge at
a merge in the *exact* same frame — is a measure-zero case the testbed avoids by staggering (the loop is
longer and the player is ahead), and is a documented deferred non-goal (§1.2/§8).

### 3.8 Render — AI trains as world meshes (`src/render/scene.ts`, impure)

AI trains are new meshes placed with `placeOnEdge(edge, s, d)` (from `graph-geom.ts`; sim→render is
allowed). The player's camera stays single-train. The only new render concept is `placeOnCentreline`
generalised to `placeOnEdge`.

### 3.9 Wiring (one paragraph)

`main.ts` builds the `TrackGraph` and `Path`s from `testbed.ts`, runs `validateGraph` once at startup
(fail-fast), holds `TrainRecord[]`, and each frame: reads the wall clock once for `dt` (the only
clock), runs the player's existing keyboard→`reduceControls`→`resolveInputs` to get `playerInputs`,
calls the pure `tickAll(...)`, runs the player's existing `tickAws`/`tickSafety` on the player record
against its **current** edge route with edge-local `(prevS, nowS)`, then renders every record via
`placeOnEdge` and updates the HUD from the player record. All logic is pure and tested; the shell has
none.

> **AWS at a join.** Like the existing single-route loop (`main.ts:122-129`), the shell captures the
> player's `prevS` before `tickAll` and feeds `(prevS, nowS)` to `tickAws` for forward-sweep crossing
> detection. On a frame that crosses a join, `nowS` is the small residual on the **new** edge while
> `prevS` is large on the old one, so `nowS ≤ prevS` makes that frame's sweep **empty** — harmless
> because the testbed's player edges carry no stations/signals (cross-join AWS-magnet continuity is the
> deferred non-goal in §1.2). D-DEC-7's "full Phase-2 machine unchanged" therefore holds *within* an
> edge; the join simply skips one sweep.

## 5. Acceptance criteria — numbered pure oracles (definition of done)

All pure (no DOM/Three/wall-clock), driven by explicit `dt` and positions. "≈" = `toBeCloseTo`.

**Frame, geometry & structural invariants**

- **P0 (identity).** Legacy route as an edge with `IDENTITY_FRAME`: `planarPoseOnEdge`/`heightOnEdge`/
  `placeOnEdge` ≈ `planarPoseAt`/`heightAt`/`placeOnCentreline` at **12 dp** for `s ∈ {0,1000,6000,…}`,
  `d ∈ {−3,0,7}`.
- **P1 (composition = O5b lifted).** Edge B with `frame = exitFrame(A)`: `planarPoseOnEdge(B,sB)` ≈ the
  hand-rotation of B's local pose by A's exit heading added to A's exit point, at **6 dp**.
- **P2 (continuity, by construction).** `validateGraph` returns no continuity defect for the testbed;
  at the merge, *both* loop and main parents share the merge edge's entry frame (≤1e-6 in (x,z), psi0,
  h0).
- **P2-NEG (teeth).** Perturb one child `frame` by `1e-3` ⇒ `validateGraph` returns a `discontinuity`
  for exactly that pair.
- **P-LEN (one join per tick).** `validateGraph` rejects a graph with an edge shorter than
  `maxSpeed·MAX_DT·SAFETY`; the testbed passes with a large margin.
- **OCC-NS (namespace disjointness).** `validateGraph` rejects a graph whose station-id set intersects
  its edge-id set; the testbed's are disjoint.
- **P3 (per-edge invariants).** Every edge: `minCurveRadius ≥ 250`; if terrain-rendered,
  `ribbonHalfWidth ≤ 0.5·minCurveRadius`; viaduct/tunnel bands wholly on κ=0; cant `0.08·|κ|·v² ≤ 0.105`.

**Join (`stepOnGraph`) — headline differential first**

- **G-DIFF (differential carry-over — primary join proof).** Build edges A and B=`exitFrame(A)`, and a
  SINGLE synthetic `Route` whose curvature/grade/speed segments are A's then B's laid end-to-end. Drive
  a train from `s=0` across the A→B boundary on the **graph** (`stepOnGraph`, `clampAtEnd=false` on A)
  and along the **concatenated route** (`step()` with the default clamp) with an identical input
  sequence; assert cumulative along-path distance **and** world pose agree to **1e-9** at every step.
  *Exactness conditions (verified against `simulation.ts`/`physics.ts`):* `acceleration()` reads only
  **grade**, so the carry/distance half is exact when the grade is **constant across the join** (the
  parent's clamped last-band grade equals the child's first-band grade); **PSR is not read by the
  dynamics** and is irrelevant to this half. The pose half is exact because the curvature bands are laid
  end-to-end identically and O5b composes them — author a **κ=0 (straight) join** so the post-boundary
  substep's clamped-tangent extrapolation on the parent equals the child's straight. *Independence
  note:* the carry/distance half is genuinely independent (the flat route does no carry arithmetic); the
  pose half is the O5b frame-composition identity, not an independent geometry implementation.
- **O-STEP-PARITY (wrapper is a no-op on the legacy case).** On a single-edge `IDENTITY_FRAME` graph
  (terminal edge), `stepOnGraph` returns **bit-identical** state to a direct `step()` (default args)
  for a fixed input sequence.
- **J1 (residual carry).** On a non-terminal edge, a train whose `step(...,false)` puts chainage at
  `edge.length + r` (r>0, speed>0) lands at `edgeId = successor`, `s ≈ r`, with speed/brakeActual/time
  carried.
- **J2 (world continuity).** `placeOnEdge(parent, parent.length, d)` ≈ `placeOnEdge(child, 0, d)` at
  **1e-6** *(follows from P2)*.
- **J3 (buffer-stop survival).** On the last edge (no successor), `stepOnGraph` uses `clampAtEnd=true`,
  so the end-stop `speed=0 at route.length` fires.

**Occupancy & AI**

- **OCC1.** `occupiedEdges` returns exactly each train's current edge.
- **OCC2 (richer-source RED).** With the player occupying block `E_main_out`, `aspectSource` for the
  looped AI omits `E_main_out`'s token, so the AI's loop-exit starter is RED; the cascade behind it is
  unchanged.
- **OCC3 (release).** When the player leaves `E_main_out`, its token reappears and the starter returns
  to YELLOW/GREEN. *(No latch touched.)*
- **AI1 (tracks PSR).** On a clear edge with limit `v`, `aiInputs` drives to `≈ v` (within hysteresis).
- **AI2 (brakes to a red).** With `aspectAhead = RED` at distance `d` on the current edge, integrating
  `stepOnGraph` forward brings the train to a stand at or before the post (`s ≤ postS`, `speed ≈ 0`).
- **AI3 (proceeds on clear).** Flip `aspectAhead` to GREEN ⇒ `speed` strictly increases.

**Multi-train & the headline scenarios**

- **MT1 (order independence).** `tickAll` over `records` in **any permutation** yields identical
  post-tick `(edgeId,s,speed)` for every train.
- **S1 (DONE — player delays AND overtakes a looped AI).** The player runs the through main while an AI
  is booked via the passing loop; the AI reaches its loop-exit starter (held RED because the player
  physically occupies `E_main_out`), brakes to a stand and waits *in the loop*, then the player passes
  and clears `E_main_out`, the starter clears, and the AI resumes and completes its Path to its
  buffer-stop edge. **Non-vacuity — the hold must be PROVEN to fire (guards against a silent no-op):**
  with the **player input sequence pinned** so the player provably occupies `E_main_out` across the AI's
  arrival ticks, assert there exists **≥1 tick** on which the AI's loop-exit aspect is **RED** *and* the
  AI `speed ≈ 0` *while* `player.pos.edgeId === "E_main_out"` — i.e. the delay genuinely happened, not
  merely that the AI eventually completed its Path. **Release:** after the player leaves `E_main_out`,
  the AI `speed > 0` resumes within K ticks and its final `pos.edgeId` is its last edge with
  `speed ≈ 0`. Deterministic, dt-driven.
- **S2 (branch divergence).** A second AI booked `[E_main_in, E_branch, E_branch_buffer]` diverges at
  the throat and runs to its buffer; `validateGraph` passes for the branch Path and the branch track
  visibly departs the main (P1/J2 on the divergent frame).

## 6. The concrete first testbed graph (`src/sim/testbed.ts`)

```
                              E_loop  (diverges, runs out & back, rejoins — LONGER than the straight)
                            ┌───────────────────────────┐
  E_main_in ──[throat J1]──┤                            ├──[merge J2]── E_main_out ── E_main_buffer
   (IDENTITY)               └───── E_main_through ───────┘                (=exitFrame E_main_through
        │                         (straight main)                          =exitFrame E_loop)
        └──── E_branch ──── E_branch_buffer
              (diverges)      (buffer stop)
```

- **E_main_in** — straight approach, ~800 m, `IDENTITY_FRAME`. Ends at throat **J1**.
- **E_main_through** — straight through main between points and merge, ~400 m, `frame =
  exitFrame(E_main_in)`. The **player** runs this.
- **E_loop** — passing-loop road, **necessarily longer than E_main_through** (an out-and-back that
  rejoins the same point with net-zero heading change must exceed the straight chord), gentle
  curvature (`R ≥ 300`). `frame = exitFrame(E_main_in)`; authored so `exitFrame(E_loop) =
  exitFrame(E_main_through)` (the merge-continuity constraint P2 enforces). Carries the loop-exit
  starter `protects = "E_main_out"`.
- **E_main_out** — through continuation past merge **J2**, ~800 m, `frame =
  exitFrame(E_main_through) = exitFrame(E_loop)`. The shared onward block the player occupies to hold
  the looped AI. Ends at **E_main_buffer** (buffer stop).
- **E_branch** — diverging branch, ~600 m, opening curve then straight, `frame = exitFrame(E_main_in)`
  with a different `psi0`/curvature. Ends at **E_branch_buffer** (buffer stop).

**Grade continuity:** edges meeting at a join share their boundary grade (a smooth railway property);
together with P-LEN this keeps the post-join residual physically faithful.

**Paths (scenario DATA):**

- **Player** (through main, overtakes): `[E_main_in, E_main_through, E_main_out, E_main_buffer]`.
- **AI-1** (looped, delayed & overtaken): `[E_main_in, E_loop, E_main_out, E_main_buffer]`.
- **AI-2** (branch divergence): `[E_main_in, E_branch, E_branch_buffer]`.

Smallest graph with a real passing loop (diverge + converge), a branch (alternate route), and exactly
the physical-occupancy interaction S1 requires. The longer loop + player-on-the-straight naturally
stagger the two so the player reaches `E_main_out` first (no same-frame merge coincidence).

## 7. Decision log (resolves all 8 open decisions)

**D-DEC-1 — Junction orientation/sign reconciliation.** *All-forward authoring → carry the residual,
keep `dir=+1`; no sign flip; reverse never crosses a join (deferred).* Oracles: G-DIFF, J1/J2.

**D-DEC-2 — `stepOnGraph` layer vs thickening `step()`.** *NEW thin pure wrapper; `step()` gains ONE
additive default-preserving parameter (`clampAtEnd`).* The wrapper suppresses the terminal clamp on a
non-buffer edge so the residual is real; every existing call site is behaviorally byte-identical
(default `true`). *Rationale (corrected from V1): a literally-unchanged `step()` cannot carry a
residual — the in-loop end-clamp zeroes speed at the boundary. One default-preserving parameter is the
narrowest honest change; the dynamics arithmetic stays untouched.* Oracles: O-STEP-PARITY, J3, G-DIFF,
the existing suite (default path).

**D-DEC-3 — Block granularity & signal kind.** *WHOLE-EDGE blocks; OVERLAY the starter cascade with
**namespaced** clear-block tokens (block token = edge id; station-ids/edge-ids disjoint, OCC-NS) — no
new signal kind.* CLEAR ⇒ token present ⇒ may show non-RED; occupied-by-another ⇒ absent ⇒ RED.
*Rationale: the brief's "richer SET SOURCE not a new primitive", with positive membership and provable
non-aliasing of the station-starter invariant.*

**D-DEC-4 — AI fidelity.** *PSR + braking-curve tracker only; no dwell/timetable.* Deferred cleanly.

**D-DEC-5 — Multi-train tick / contention.** *Occupancy is PHYSICAL (current edge only), sampled once
from frame-N positions ⇒ order-independent (MT1). No reservation, no tie-break, no priority field.*
*Rationale (corrected from V1): reservation + the lower-trainId tie-break defended a same-frame
double-entry race the testbed never exercises — an on-ramp to nowhere. Physical occupancy delivers S1
and S2; the measure-zero merge coincidence is a documented deferred non-goal.*

**D-DEC-6 — Node geometry.** *Pure frame hand-off; divergence baked into the branch child's curvature;
merge continuity enforced by `validateGraph`.* No turnout mesh, no blade state.

**D-DEC-7 — AI signalling.** *Reduced — reads the aspect, obeys a braking curve; NO AWS/TPWS/DSD.*
Keeps the Phase-2 penalty/latch machine player-only and untouched.

**D-DEC-8 — `TrainPosition.d`.** *Parallel running is TWO DISTINCT EDGES; `d` is a constant per-train
render offset only.* One concept per axis: `edgeId` routes, `s` advances, `d` nudges the mesh.

## 8. Residual risks

1. **Join correctness depends on all-forward authoring.** *Mitigation:* P2 + G-DIFF catch any frame
   discontinuity; `validateGraph` is the guard. Reverse-through-joins deferred to keep this true.
2. **Whole-edge, point-train occupancy is coarse.** A train is a point at `s`, so (a) sub-edge headway
   is not modelled and (b) the **terminal buffer edge has no protecting starter, so a released AI may
   co-occupy `E_main_buffer` nose-to-tail behind a standing player** — an accepted consequence of
   point-train whole-edge blocks in this increment, not a surprise (the buffer is a dead end).
   *Mitigation:* acceptable for the testbed; sub-interval blocks are a later "more data, no new concept"
   extension, and a one-line scenario-data starter protecting `E_main_buffer`'s approach would add
   rear-end protection using the same namespaced-token mechanism if ever wanted.
3. **Same-frame double-entry at a merge** (two trains entering one empty edge in the exact same frame)
   is not resolved by physical-only occupancy. *Mitigation:* the testbed staggers them (longer loop +
   player ahead) so it cannot arise; reservation is the deferred fix if a future scenario needs it.
4. **Post-join residual uses the parent edge's end grade** (sub-metre, sub-tick). *Mitigation:* grade
   continuity at joins (authoring property) + P-LEN keep it negligible; G-DIFF uses constant-grade edges
   to stay exact.
5. **AI bang-bang may chatter** near the target/knee. *Mitigation:* the hysteresis band; a single-pole
   filter is a contained follow-up. Determinism unaffected.
6. **Merge geometry is demanding** (the loop must rejoin the through edge's exit pose). *Mitigation:* P2
   enforces it numerically; the author solves the loop curvature.
7. **Render perf with N AI meshes** is outside the tested core; the increment ships two AI trains —
   a non-issue now, bounded later by mesh reuse.

### Appendix — the seven new pure concepts, in one breath

`EdgeFrame` (un-hardwire the integration origin) → `Edge` (a Route read through a frame) → `TrackGraph`
(a bag of edges joined by `exit==entry`, checked by `validateGraph`) → `TrainPosition {edgeId,s,d}`
(where a train is) → `Path` (the booked edge list) → derived **physical** `occupancy` (namespaced
clear-block tokens folded into the unchanged `aspectAt`) → `aiInputs` (a target-speed tracker emitting
`SimInputs`) → `stepOnGraph` (carry the residual across a join over a `clampAtEnd`-parameterised
`step()`). Nothing else. The asset — `step()`'s dynamics, `acceleration`, the AWS/TPWS/DSD machine, the
latch-release contract, every existing oracle — is reused verbatim (one additive default-preserving
parameter aside).
